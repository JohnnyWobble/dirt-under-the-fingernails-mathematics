Dirt Under the Fingernails Mathematics

This year, at the 2021 Virginia Summer Residential Governor’s School for Mathematics, Science and Technology at University of Lynchburg, 20 of us elected to enroll in Dirt Under the Fingernail Mathematics, and not one of us regretted it. Taught by Dr. Ordower, this was the most fascinating math course any of us had ever taken. Requiring nothing more than algebra, basic pre-calculus, and a love for math, Dr. Ordower designed a curriculum to teach through a variety of games, puzzles, and hands on activities, that amazed us with new concepts and ideas every day.

Throughout the course we learned about systems which evolve over time, combinatorial game theory, number theory, graph theory, and cellular automaton. Throughout the course, Dr. Ordower had a very dynamic curriculum allowing us to spend days exploring niche interests and journeying down rabbit holes.

Class trip to Randolph College which included a tour of the Maier Art Museum

Overall, everyone agrees that the class was fantastic, everyday brought a new discovery. As one student said, “it was like relearning basic arithmetic and algebra”. The class wholeheartedly agrees that Dr. Ordower is one of the best teachers they’ve ever had. He is extremely enthusiastic, incredibly knowledgeable, and super nice. All things considered, this was the best class we've ever taken.
